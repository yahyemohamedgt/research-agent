# Twitter/X content is synthesized, not verbatim — split into x_signals

Grok (the Twitter/X collector's synthesis model) summarizes tweet text even when the prompt explicitly asks for verbatim quotes. That two-level drift — tweet → Grok summary → Sonnet "verbatim" — means Twitter content can never be verified against the corpus the way Reddit, Exa, YouTube, and the rest can. Rather than keep pretending these are quotes, Twitter/X items get their own `x_signals` field on the Brief, honestly labeled as Grok-synthesized signal, and are excluded from `verbatim_phrases` and from the hallucination check. Enforcement lives in code, not just the prompt: `synthesize()` in `graph.py` moves any `verbatim_phrases` entry whose URL matches `x.com/` or `twitter.com/` into `x_signals` after the tool call returns (`_is_twitter_url()`), because the prompt alone didn't reliably keep the model from putting Twitter content in the verbatim field.
